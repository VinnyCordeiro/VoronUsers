# Carbon Fiber gantry for the V0.0 (and maybe the V0.1?)

Took the inspiration from [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X4FvWHkUu8M).

Let's face it: the \#SpeedBoatRace that was proposed at the end of 2020 started a number of really cool inovations on the 3d printing community, where people wants to print fast *and* with quality. One thing that helps getting ludicrous speeds and accelerations is lowering the mass of the gantry, and there is no better material for that than carbon fiber reinforced polymer. So I've designed a drilling/slot pattern for a 15mm × 15mm × 200mm square carbon fiber tube and a printed piece that will hold M2 heat inserts and M3 captive nuts channels. The 200mm carbon fiber square tube is 4.3× lighter than a 200mm long 1515 Misumi aluminum profile, from a Fusion 360 simulation.
